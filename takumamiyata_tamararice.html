The Mission

My root is "to spread true French cuisine in Japan" in a real sense.

While working at elite restaurants like Imperial Hotel and Quintessence, I felt a disconnect. Friends would ask, "What actually IS French cuisine?" I realized that unlike Italian or Asian cuisines, French has not permeated daily life in Japan. The common thread in dishes recognized by Japanese people is "Carbohydrates." Italian steak (Bistecca) isn't widely known, but pasta is. French bread never became a meal-mate for the Japanese table like rice. That's why I focus on Rice to increase the "contact rate" and build the foundation of French culture.
Just as Mapo Tofu became a staple thanks to the base created by household brands before authentic Sichuan styles arrived, I want to create the "Base" for French cuisine. I want a future where someone asks "What's for dinner?" and the answer is "Coq au Vin!" as naturally as saying "Curry".
